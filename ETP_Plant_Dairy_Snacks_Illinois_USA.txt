2.1 TYPICAL WASTEWATER CHARACTERISTICS
----------------------------------------
Parameter         | Typical Raw Value  | Discharge Limit (POTW)
------------------|--------------------|------------------------
BOD               | 1,000–5,000 mg/L   | < 250 mg/L
COD               | 2,000–8,000 mg/L   | < 500 mg/L
TSS               | 300–1,500 mg/L     | < 250 mg/L
FOG               | 200–1,000 mg/L     | < 100 mg/L
pH                | 4–11 (wide swing)  | 6.0–9.0
TKN (Nitrogen)    | 50–150 mg/L        | < 40 mg/L
Total Phosphorus  | 20–80 mg/L         | < 10 mg/L
Temperature       | 30–45°C            | < 40°C
Flow Rate         | 10,000–500,000 gpd | As per permit

Primary Sources of Wastewater:
  - CIP (Clean-in-Place) wash water (HIGH volume, HIGH pH swings)
  - Product spills (milk, whey, cream, yogurt)
  - Pasteurizer/separator/homogenizer washdown
  - Boiler blowdown & cooling tower blowdown
  - Floor washing / sanitation

2.2 RECOMMENDED TREATMENT TRAIN — DAIRY
-----------------------------------------

STAGE 1 → PRELIMINARY TREATMENT
  [Screen & Grit Chamber]
  - Bar screen (6–10 mm): Remove large solids, packaging debris
  - Fine screen (1–2 mm): Capture fine food particles
  - Grit chamber: Remove sand/silt from floor wash

STAGE 2 → EQUALIZATION
  [EQ Tank — 6–12 hr HRT]
  - CRITICAL for dairy — pH swings from CIP (pH 2–13)
  - Blends acid/alkaline CIP streams before treatment
  - Flow equalization prevents shock loads on downstream units
  - Equipped with mixing, pH probe, level control

STAGE 3 → PRIMARY TREATMENT
  [DAF — Dissolved Air Flotation]
  - Removes 70–90% FOG (fat, oil, grease)
  - Removes 50–70% TSS
  - Removes 30–40% BOD
  - Chemical dosing: Coagulant (Alum/FeCl3) + Polymer
  - Scum (FOG sludge) sent to sludge handling
  - Recycle ratio: 25–50%
  - HRT: 20–40 minutes

STAGE 4 → SECONDARY TREATMENT (ANAEROBIC)
  [UASB Reactor or Anaerobic MBBR]
  - Suitable when COD > 3,000 mg/L
  - COD removal: 60–80%
  - Biogas recovery: 0.35 m³ CH4 per kg COD removed
    (Can power 20–30% of plant energy needs)
  - HRT: 4–8 hours (UASB)
  - Not required if discharging to POTW with lenient limits

STAGE 5 → SECONDARY TREATMENT (AEROBIC)
  Option A: MBR (Membrane Bioreactor)
    - BOD removal: >98%
    - TSS effluent: <5 mg/L
    - Compact footprint — best for land-constrained sites
    - Higher CAPEX, lower OPEX (no clarifier needed)
    - Suitable for direct NPDES discharge

  Option B: SBR (Sequencing Batch Reactor)
    - BOD removal: 90–95%
    - Flexible for variable flows
    - Nitrification/denitrification in single tank
    - Lower CAPEX vs MBR
    - 4–6 hour cycle time

  Option C: Extended Aeration (Activated Sludge)
    - Simple, robust, operator-friendly
    - BOD removal: 85–95%
    - Requires secondary clarifier
    - Best for: plants with experienced operators, larger land

STAGE 6 → TERTIARY TREATMENT (if NPDES direct discharge)
  - Sand/Multi-media filtration
  - UV disinfection or Chlorination
  - Phosphorus removal (chemical precipitation with alum)
  - Ammonia removal (air stripping or biological nitrification)

STAGE 7 → SLUDGE MANAGEMENT
  - Thickener → Belt Filter Press or Centrifuge
  - Sludge cake: 15–25% DS
  - Disposal: Land application (if meets 40 CFR 503) or landfill
  - Biosolids testing required quarterly

3.1 TYPICAL WASTEWATER CHARACTERISTICS
----------------------------------------
Parameter         | Fried Snacks        | Baked Snacks
------------------|---------------------|------------------
BOD               | 1,500–4,000 mg/L    | 500–2,000 mg/L
COD               | 2,500–6,000 mg/L    | 800–3,000 mg/L
TSS               | 300–1,000 mg/L      | 150–600 mg/L
FOG               | 200–800 mg/L        | 50–200 mg/L
Starch (as COD)   | High                | Very High
pH                | 6–8                 | 5–8
Flow Rate         | 5,000–200,000 gpd   | 5,000–150,000 gpd

Primary Sources:
  - Fryer oil carryover (chips, extruded snacks)
  - Starch/dough residue from extruder/mixer washdown
  - Seasoning/flavoring washdown (salt, spices, MSG)
  - CIP cleaning of processing equipment
  - Blancher/cooling water (potato chips)

3.2 RECOMMENDED TREATMENT TRAIN — SNACKS
-----------------------------------------

STAGE 1 → PRETREATMENT
  [Grease Trap / FOG Interceptor]
  - MANDATORY for frying operations (IL POTW requirement)
  - Size: 1,000–5,000 gallon (as per local POTW specs)
  - Captures bulk fats & cooking oils
  - Pumped out weekly by licensed hauler

  [Fine Screening 1–3 mm]
  - Removes starch solids, crumb, food particles
  - Rotary drum or wedge wire screen

STAGE 2 → EQUALIZATION
  [EQ Tank — 4–8 hr HRT]
  - Flow and load averaging
  - pH adjustment (neutralization)
  - Mixing to prevent starch settling

STAGE 3 → PRIMARY TREATMENT
  [DAF Unit]
  - Removes residual FOG after grease trap
  - FOG removal: 80–90%
  - TSS removal: 60–75%
  - Starch/suspended solids captured in float sludge

STAGE 4 → SECONDARY TREATMENT
  Option A: SBR (Recommended for Snacks)
    - Handles variable/seasonal production well
    - BOD removal: 90–95%
    - Single tank for aeration + settling
    - Easy to operate semi-automatically

  Option B: MBBR (Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor)
    - Good for high starch loads
    - More resilient to shock loads
    - Compact, no return sludge needed
    - BOD removal: 85–95%

  Option C: Activated Sludge + Clarifier
    - Traditional, proven technology
    - Requires experienced operator
    - Low capital for large flows

STAGE 5 → POLISHING (if NPDES)
  - Multimedia filter
  - UV disinfection
  - Phosphorus precipitation

STAGE 6 → SLUDGE HANDLING
  - DAF float + biological sludge → thickener
  - Dewatering: Belt press / screw press
  - Starch-rich sludge → composting potential
  - Disposal: 40 CFR 503 land application or landfill

SECTION 7: DECISION GUIDE — WHICH ETP FOR YOU?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q1: Are you discharging to municipal sewer (POTW)?
    → YES: Pretreatment only needed (DAF + pH + EQ)
           Estimated CAPEX: $100K–$500K
    → NO : Full NPDES ETP required
           Estimated CAPEX: $500K–$5M+

Q2: Is your flow > 100,000 gpd?
    → YES: MBR or MBBR + Anaerobic preferred
    → NO : SBR or Extended Aeration sufficient

Q3: Is energy recovery important?
    → YES: Include UASB/AnMBR (biogas to boiler/CHP)
    → NO : Skip anaerobic, go straight aerobic

Q4: Is land (footprint) a constraint?
    → YES: MBR (smallest footprint, no clarifier)
    → NO : SBR or Activated Sludge (lower cost)

Q5: Snacks — do you fry (chips/extruded)?
    → YES: Grease trap + DAF mandatory
    → NO : Simple aerobic system may suffice
